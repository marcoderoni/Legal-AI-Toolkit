# Workflow: Email / Teams Response Drafting

## Objective
Draft a professional response to a counterparty email or Teams message
containing contract objections, redline requests, or negotiation points.
Output is ready to send or minimally edited before sending.

## Required Input
- The original email or message, pasted in full
- Any relevant context (deal stage, relationship, prior agreed points)
- Tone preference if different from default (see below)

## Step 1 — Parse the Incoming Message
Identify:
- What are they asking or objecting to?
- How many distinct points are raised?
- What is the tone — aggressive, collaborative, procedural?
- Is there a deadline or urgency signal?

## Step 2 — Check Positions
For each point raised, check:
1. references/playbook.docx — is there an established position?
2. Apply decision framework:
   - Fallback available → use it
   - No fallback + low risk → propose language
   - No fallback + high risk → flag for escalation, do not respond yet

## Step 3 — Draft the Response
Structure:

**Opening**: acknowledge receipt, set collaborative tone

**Point-by-point response**: address each of their points in the same
order they raised them. For each:
- State our position clearly
- Offer the fallback or counter-proposal where available
- Avoid "no" without an alternative — always propose something

**Closing**: next step, timeline if relevant
